Looking at Good Examples of Site Design

AUTHOR | April 2, 2023 | 5 minute read

It is not easy designing a good website. Anyone can load up an HTML file and host it to the web even if they have to follow a tutorial to do so. However, making that blank page online appear in an effective way for the reason you uploaded it in the first place is a different story!

In this article we'll look at a few different types of websites and the reason why they are effectively designed. Here's a list of the types of sites we'll be looking into:
  • Portfolios
  • Business sites
  • Organizational sites
  • One-page websites

What are the basics of good web design?

Like anything complicated, there is a basic set of rules that should be generally followed when designing for the web. These rules and a more in-depth explanation of each can be seen in this article by Adobe.

Here is a list and short description of a few of the rules:

  • Keep things consistent
  • Make navigating easy
  • Change the color of visited links
  • Make your pages quick and easy to read
  • ...

Looking at some sites for design principles

A portfolio

Portfolio websites need to adhere closely to design principles while also stretching the standard design to match their personal branding. This is a careful line that is difficult to get just right.


For a solid example of getting this balance just right, look no further than this portfolio site by Gary Shear. Scroll through the different examples using the arrows or visit the site using the links.

If you navigate through the gallery, you can immediately see a fantastic example of uniform design. There is a careful attention to symmetry and uniform styles throughout the whole site.

From the headings to the images, every element is carefully positioned and placed to maintain a uniform design. Keeping everything uniform on your page is a must and fantastic way to improve many key areas in your design.
