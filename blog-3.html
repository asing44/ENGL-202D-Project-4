What is Storyboarding?

ADAM WARSING | April 3, 2023 | 5 minute read

The basis for any design project is good planning. A classic step in designing for the web is using storyboards and storyboarding. Storyboarding is the act of breaking down a story or script into a series of images that show the narrative of the story or project. If you are unfarmiliar with the term or confused by the definition, that's okay, you're in a great place to learn more!

Here are some common places other than web design that storyboarding is utilized:
  • Filmmaking
  • Animation
  • Videography
  • ...

Why to Create Storyboards

In essence, creating storyboards makes the process from concept to design much easier. It allows you to start piecing together the bigger picture, literally. We'll start by taking a deeper look into some of the main reasons why storyboarding is useful.


Sharing ideas

Storyboards allow for you to quickly share ideas to others. It is one thing to describe a scene to someone but to show them the scene and establish a general understanding of how the scene came about, is much more effective.


Others will become much more interested in your ideas if they can understand them! You can help others understand your ideas better with storyboards.


Makes designing easier

When you storyboard you are easing into the design process. Instead of spending hours on a scene, trying to make it production-ready, you only spend a few minutes. You don't get final quality out of doing this, you get a view into whether you intial design decisions are good or not.

It is much easier to redesign a storyboard that you made in under an hour than to redesign a whole page on your website. Once you decide on a storyboard scene or section you like, you can develop it further. Storyboarding is not a one-and-done type of activity! The use comes from the ability to generate and redo designing quickly. Think of it like testing out your ideas.


Steps to create a storyboard

Now that we have the basics of storyboarding down, lets look into a general way you can make a storyboard. You can adjust the design to meet whatever you are creating!

Here are the steps:

  1. Create your sections. You can do this by folding a piece of paper into 8 equal parts. For each section, include an area to draw out the scene. Also include a place to write a quick summary of the scene.
  2. Add sene descriptions. Start the drawing by writing descriptions for each of your panels.
  3. Draw your scenes. Now, draw your scenes and tie them into the descriptions you already wrote.

If you want even more information on storyboards, check out this article written on boords.com.

Congrats! You have just learned how to storyboard and maybe even created your own storyboard! There are meny ways you can (and should) brainstorm a design.

The design process starts with planning and it crucial to get the planning right! Careful planning makes the rest of the design process much, much easier and you will be thankful you held off on jumping immediately into the design.
